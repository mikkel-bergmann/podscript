# Trademark & naming

The licenses in this repository (see [`LICENSE.md`](LICENSE.md)) grant broad rights to
**copy, implement, and adapt** the Podscript specification and its conformance
fixtures. They do **not** grant rights to the **"Podscript" name**, which is handled
separately here.

## What you may do

- Build and distribute your own Podscript implementation (parser, resolver, renderer,
  tooling), including commercially, under any license you choose.
- State that your software **"implements Podscript"**, is **"Podscript-compatible"**, or
  **"conforms to the Podscript specification vX.Y"** — factual, descriptive use is fine.

## What we ask you not to do

- Don't name your product, library, or service **"Podscript"** (or a confusingly similar
  name) in a way that implies it is the official project or is endorsed by it.
- Don't claim **conformance** unless your implementation passes the relevant
  `conformance/` fixtures for the class(es) you target (parser / resolver / renderer).

The goal is simple: keep "Podscript" meaning *the specification and conforming
implementations*, so the name stays a reliable signal for users — while leaving everyone
free to implement the format however they like.

> This is a lightweight community naming policy, not a registered trademark claim or
> legal advice. If the project formalizes a trademark later, this document will be
> updated.

Questions about naming or endorsement: open an issue.
